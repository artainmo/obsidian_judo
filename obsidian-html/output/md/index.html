Movement

When moving around always keep feet close to the ground, never raise them too high else you end up in a more vulnerable position. And donâ€™t make small steps, but instead sweep them over the floor.
Also, always have knees slightly bend, donâ€™t keep legs straight, this keeps you more stable on legs.

When moving opponent around use legs and own body weight before arms. Keep arms mainly loose and move around until an opportunity arises.
If constantly locking arms and using strength you can avoid them attacking you but you cannot attack either and you let them feel where you are moving which allows them to know where your momentum lies and thus what movements they can down you with. Thus in general you should not lock arms but occasionally it can be used as a defensive technique. Locking arms and bending, pulling down, is I believe a defensive technique when in danger. Pushing opponent can put him in a forward momentum if he resists. Similarly, pulling can put opponent in a backward momentum if he tries to resist. Those short-lived momentums can be followed by an appropriate technique.
